Live sessions

Pre-recorded talks raise needs, questions and suggestions for standardization work, captured as GitHub issues.

Three live sessions are scheduled during the week of 15-19 November 2021 to review these issues and converge on a common understanding of whether and how they may progress from a standardization perspective.

The calls are open to all workshop participants. If you did not receive calendar invites with meeting information, please contact François Daoust <fd@w3.org>.

Familiarity with workshop talks and issues is recommended. There will be no speakers and presentations during these calls. We will rather ask participants to take an active role and help shape next steps for each of the topics.

Live session 1: Encoded/Decoded Media Frames

When: Monday 15 November - 4pm UTC - 90mn (see local time conversion)

This session will cover gaps and needs linked to manipulating individual media frames in media production workflows, including:

  • WebCodecs needs
  • Web Audio needs
  • Synchronization issues

Live session 2: Transport, Processing, Storage

When: Tuesday 16 November - 11pm UTC - 90mn (see local time conversion)

This session will cover gaps and needs linked to media ingest, transport, processing and local storage in media production workflows, including:

  • WebRTC needs
  • WebAssembly needs
  • Storage needs

Live session 3: Architecture, Next Steps

When: Friday 19 November - 3pm UTC - 90mn (see local time conversion)

This session will review more architectural-level issues in media production workflows, summarize workshop inputs and create an action plan for standardization:

  • Metadata maintenance throughout production workflows
  • Privacy and other architectural considerations
  • Impact of accessibility needs on authoring tools and produced media assets
  • Next steps: standardization roadmap
